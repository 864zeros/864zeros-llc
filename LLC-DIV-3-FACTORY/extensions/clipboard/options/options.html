How to use ClipBoard
  1. Open the side panel — Click the ClipBoard icon in your toolbar, or right-click any page
  2. Capture content — Select text and click the + button, or use the capture menu for screenshots and full pages
  3. Organize with tags — Add tags manually or let AI suggest them for you
  4. Search everything — Use the search bar to find clips by content, title, URL, or tags
  5. Export anytime — Your data is yours. Export to JSON or sync to Google Drive (Pro)

Privacy & Your Data

AI features. Your Gemini API key is stored only on this device, in chrome.storage.local. It is sent only to Google's official Gemini endpoint at generativelanguage.googleapis.com when you trigger an AI analysis. 864zeros never sees your key, your prompts, or your responses.

Google Drive sync (Pro). Authentication uses Google's OAuth flow via chrome.identity.launchWebAuthFlow(). You sign in with your own Google account; clipboard never sees your password. The OAuth access token is stored in chrome.storage.local on this device only. All Drive API calls go directly from your browser to Google.

Payments (Pro). ClipBoard uses ExtensionPay (extensionpay.com) to process payments. ExtPay handles credit card data directly; 864zeros never sees your payment details. 864zeros receives payment-status metadata (e.g., "user X paid for Pro tier") via ExtPay's standard merchant flow.

Captured content. Your clips (text, screenshots, PDFs, tags, AI summaries) live in IndexedDB on this device. They do not leave your device unless you explicitly trigger an action that uses an external feature (Drive sync, AI analysis, or the upcoming Sovereign History export).

The "debugger" permission. Used only to generate PDF exports of pages you choose (the "Save as PDF" feature). When you click Save as PDF, ClipBoard briefly attaches Chrome's DevTools Protocol to the active tab, captures the page as a PDF, and immediately detaches. The PDF goes to your local IndexedDB or Downloads folder. No data is ever sent over the network through this permission.

The "management" permission. Used only to enumerate which other extensions you have installed (for compatibility/conflict detection). Does not transmit anything.
